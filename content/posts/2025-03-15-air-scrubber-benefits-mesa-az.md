Most Mesa homeowners don't realize that the air inside their home can be **up to 5 times more polluted** than the air outside, according to the EPA. Traditional air filters catch particles as air passes through your HVAC system, but they can't address pollutants that settle on surfaces, gas-phase contaminants like VOCs, or biological threats like viruses and bacteria. Air scrubbers fill this critical gap by actively purifying both the air and surfaces throughout your entire home.

## How Air Scrubbers Work

Unlike passive air filters that wait for contaminated air to pass through them, air scrubbers use **advanced photocatalytic oxidation (PCO)** technology to actively send purifying molecules throughout your home. Installed directly in your HVAC ductwork, the unit uses a UV light and catalytic surface to generate oxidizers that:

- Travel through your ductwork into every room
- Neutralize airborne contaminants in the air
- Clean surfaces where pathogens settle (countertops, doorknobs, tables)
- Break down odor-causing compounds at the molecular level

This active approach means air scrubbers work even when your HVAC system is between cycles, providing continuous whole-home purification.

## 10 Benefits Arizona Homeowners Don't Expect

### 1. Removes Up to 99% of Airborne Contaminants

Air scrubbers eliminate bacteria, viruses, mold spores, pollen, and fine particulate matter that pass through standard filters. For Mesa families with allergy sufferers, asthmatics, or immunocompromised members, this level of purification can dramatically improve daily comfort and health.

### 2. Cleans Surfaces — Not Just Air

Traditional filtration only works on airborne particles. Air scrubbers send purifying molecules that settle on surfaces throughout your home, reducing bacteria and viruses on countertops, light switches, doorknobs, and other high-touch areas. This is especially valuable during cold and flu season.

### 3. Eliminates Odors at the Molecular Level

Cooking smells, pet odors, cleaning chemical residue, and musty HVAC system odors are broken down at the molecular level — not just masked by fragrances. The result is genuinely fresh-smelling air rather than perfumed air hiding underlying odors.

### 4. Reduces Desert Dust Impact

Mesa's desert environment means fine particulate matter infiltrates your home constantly through door openings, gaps, and even your HVAC system's fresh air intake. Air scrubbers reduce the impact of Arizona's dust-heavy air quality, helping sensitive family members breathe more comfortably.

### 5. Fights Monsoon-Season Mold

Arizona's monsoon season (June-September) introduces moisture that can fuel mold growth in dark, damp areas of your home — particularly around HVAC drain pans, ductwork, and bathroom areas. Air scrubbers neutralize mold spores before they can colonize and spread.

### 6. Extends HVAC System Life

By reducing the biological and particulate load your HVAC system handles, air scrubbers help keep internal components cleaner. Cleaner coils, blower motors, and ductwork mean less strain on your system, potentially extending its operational life.

### 7. Reduces Cleaning Frequency

With fewer airborne particles settling on surfaces, many homeowners notice they need to dust less frequently after air scrubber installation. While not a replacement for regular cleaning, the reduction in settled dust and biological contaminants is a welcome daily benefit.

### 8. Complements Your Existing Filtration

Air scrubbers work alongside your existing HVAC filter — not as a replacement. Your filter catches large particles (dust, pet hair, lint), while the air scrubber handles fine particles, biological contaminants, gases, and odors that filters can't address. Together, they provide comprehensive air quality management.

### 9. Operates Silently and Automatically

Once installed, air scrubbers require no daily attention. They operate silently within your ductwork, activating automatically when your HVAC system runs. The only maintenance is annual UV lamp replacement during your regular HVAC tune-up.

### 10. Protects Your Family During Illness Season

When a family member is sick, an air scrubber reduces the spread of airborne pathogens throughout your home. Instead of recirculating virus-laden air through your ductwork to every room, the scrubber actively neutralizes biological contaminants, helping protect other family members.
